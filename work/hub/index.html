The Problem

A platform with great content that nobody used.

The Hub had years of high-quality financial content — but the experience made it nearly impossible to find, personalize, or act on any of it.

User Problem

No clear path forward

  • Users landed on a generic dashboard with no personalization
  • Content was buried in category menus, not surfaced contextually
  • No connection between learning, action steps, and real outcomes
  • Mobile experience was an afterthought — causing drop-off on smaller screens

Business Problem

Engagement metrics told the real story

  • High bounce rates on the main dashboard
  • Employers couldn't prove ROI on the wellness program
  • The platform felt like a content library, not a coaching product

The Approach

Design around the user's financial journey, not the content catalog.

The redesign required rethinking the platform from first principles — who the user is when they arrive, what they actually need, and how to get them to their next meaningful financial step.

01

Personalized entry points

Built an onboarding flow that captures financial priorities upfront, so every session starts with contextual relevance — not a generic home screen.

02

Journey-based navigation

Replaced category menus with guided financial journeys — debt, retirement, emergency fund, benefits — giving users a clear path from awareness to action.

03

Behavioral design principles

Applied progress mechanics, goal anchoring, and bite-sized content modules to reduce cognitive load and increase return visits and session depth.

04

Mobile-first execution

Rebuilt the responsive system from scratch — ensuring the experience worked as well on a phone as on a desktop, meeting employees where they actually are.

Learnings

What I'd tell a designer starting this project.

  • 01

    Engagement is a design problem, not a content problem.

    The platform had excellent content but poor engagement. The issue was always structural — how information was organized, surfaced, and sequenced — not what was inside it.

  • 02

    Personalization at entry changes everything downstream.

    Capturing a user's priorities upfront meant every subsequent interaction could be framed around what actually mattered to them — transforming a generic library into a personalized coaching experience.

  • 03

    Ship something real. Demos earn more than decks.

    Delivering a functional demo rather than a prototype or slide deck accelerated stakeholder buy-in and engineering alignment significantly. Seeing it work made the vision real in a way static designs never could.
