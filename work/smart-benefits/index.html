The Problem

The most important financial decision of the year — made in 20 minutes with no guidance.

Open enrollment is the one moment when employees can make or break their financial wellness for the entire year. Yet most platforms treat it as a form-filling exercise, not a decision support moment.

User Problem

Complexity without context

  • Employees don't understand the difference between plan tiers, deductibles, or HSA eligibility
  • No way to model how different choices affect take-home pay or out-of-pocket costs
  • Most employees re-enroll in last year's selections without reviewing changes
  • Missed supplemental benefits — like FSA, disability, or life insurance — that could have real impact

Business Problem

Employers couldn't prove benefits ROI

  • Low engagement with voluntary benefits meant employers were paying for programs employees didn't use
  • Recordkeeper platforms had no guidance layer — they showed plans but didn't explain them
  • A white-label AI widget could fill this gap at scale, without disrupting existing enrollment systems

The Approach

Build a guidance layer, not a replacement system.

Smart Benefits was designed to sit on top of existing enrollment platforms — not replace them. The goal was a lightweight, embeddable experience that could surface the right guidance at the right moment in the enrollment flow.

01

Situation-first personalization

A short intake flow captures life situation, family size, and health usage — so recommendations are grounded in the employee's actual context, not generic plan rankings.

02

Plain-language explanations

Every benefit type — HSA, FSA, HMO, PPO — is explained in plain, jargon-free language. Employees understand what they're choosing before they choose it.

03

Embeddable at any touchpoint

Smart Benefits is a white-label widget that embeds directly into recordkeeper portals, HR platforms, or intranet pages — meeting employees in the enrollment interface they already use.

04

CFP-backed, no open internet

Like all Financial Finesse products, Smart Benefits draws from expert CFP guidance only — no hallucinated recommendations, no generic financial advice from unverified sources.

Learnings

What I'd tell a designer starting this project.

  • 01

    The best guidance is invisible until you need it.

    Smart Benefits works because it doesn't interrupt the enrollment flow — it enhances it. The most impactful design decision was making the widget feel like a natural part of the experience, not an add-on.

  • 02

    Plain language is a product feature, not a content afterthought.

    Employees don't fail at benefits enrollment because they're not trying — they fail because the language is inaccessible. Investing in plain-language design had more impact than any UI improvement.

  • 03

    White-label products require designing for flexibility first.

    Every employer has a different brand, platform, and plan structure. Building a design system that could adapt without breaking required a fundamentally different approach than designing a fixed product.
