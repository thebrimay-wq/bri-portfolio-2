Creative Director & Product Designer
Based in California

I design digital experiences that feel honest, considered, and genuinely useful — across product design, brand systems, marketing websites, and AI-powered platforms. My work lives at the intersection of clarity and craft, whether I'm shaping how a product works, how a brand shows up across the web, or how a company tells its story.

At Financial Finesse, I've led design across product, brand, and digital storytelling — shaping everything from AI-driven platform experiences to the marketing site, visual systems, and how the brand shows up across the full digital ecosystem. It's work that requires strategic thinking, behavioral insight, and real craft, all in service of making complex financial wellness feel approachable and human.

I care about the details most people never notice — the micro-interaction that reduces anxiety, the copy that actually answers the question, the flow that makes someone feel capable instead of confused. I'm especially drawn to work where the product experience and the brand ecosystem evolve together, where the same thinking that shapes a user flow also informs the marketing page, the visual language, and the story being told.

What I Lead

I work across the full design spectrum — from product UX and AI interfaces to marketing websites, brand systems, and visual storytelling. I'm equally comfortable defining a product's interaction model, art directing a brand campaign, or building out a design system from scratch. The common thread is always the same: make it clear, make it feel right, and make it genuinely work for the people using it.

What I Believe

Values that shape the work.

01

Clarity over cleverness

The best designs feel obvious in hindsight. I chase that feeling — where complexity dissolves into something that just makes sense.

02

Momentum matters

Great products build confidence. Every interaction should leave someone feeling more capable, not less certain about what to do next.

03

Craft at every scale

The details matter — even in enterprise software, even in financial tools. Polish signals respect for the person using it.
